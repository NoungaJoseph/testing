# Enako Outreach Strategic Plan 2026-2028

## 1) Vision
Every child and family in our focus communities can access education, clean water, and basic health support without financial or geographic barriers.

## 2) Mission
Deliver practical, measurable, community-led programs in Cameroon through trusted partnerships with schools, local leaders, and volunteers.

## 3) Strategic Objectives
1. Education Access
- Expand scholarship support across primary, secondary, and university tracks.
- Strengthen school partnerships and student retention.
2. Teacher Development
- Recognize high-performing teachers and scale teacher support incentives.
3. Water Security
- Deploy and maintain clean water points in underserved communities.
4. Health Access
- Run targeted community health outreach with prevention-first campaigns.
5. Women and Youth Livelihoods
- Provide skills training and starter support for vulnerable women and youth.

## 4) Annual Targets
### 2026
- Support 120+ students and deepen quality support in existing partner schools.
- Deliver at least 2 clean-water projects and 2 recurring health outreach rounds.
- Complete baseline mapping in 8 target communities.

### 2027
- Increase student support coverage by 30%.
- Expand school and teacher program footprint to new local partners.
- Launch structured youth employability and entrepreneurship cohorts.

### 2028
- Consolidate operations into a replicable regional model.
- Publish outcome dashboard and partner performance scorecards.
- Build continuity plans with local institutions for long-term sustainability.

## 5) Program Model
1. Assess needs with local stakeholders.
2. Co-design interventions with community leadership.
3. Implement with transparent budget tracking.
4. Monitor outcomes monthly and adjust quickly.
5. Publish annual progress and lessons learned.

## 6) Monitoring and Evaluation (M&E)
- Core indicators: beneficiaries reached, retention, access improvements, and outcome quality.
- Data cadence: monthly operations review, quarterly partner review, annual public report.
- Quality checks: field verification, beneficiary feedback loops, and risk mitigation reviews.

## 7) Risk Management
- Funding volatility: diversify donor channels and stagger project phases.
- Access constraints: build local implementation teams and contingency workflows.
- Program drift: enforce KPI checkpoints and governance sign-offs.

## 8) Partnership Strategy
- Schools, community committees, health actors, and local government liaison points.
- Clear roles, simple reporting templates, and shared implementation calendars.

## 9) Financial Discipline
- Priority budgeting tied to outcomes.
- Dedicated reserves for urgent response and continuity.
- Transparent reporting with periodic partner-facing summaries.

## 10) Delivery Governance
- Program leads: Education, Water, Health, Community Livelihoods.
- Monthly leadership review and quarterly strategic review cycle.
- Corrective action plans for underperforming workstreams.

## 11) Summary
This plan prioritizes impact quality over expansion speed, while creating a stable path for sustainable scale across focus communities.
